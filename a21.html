Remote vs. Office

An Exploration of Modern Work Environments

The Global Debate

Certain countries allow their workers to have a remote job, while some do not let their paid workers work remotely. This writing will highlight both pros and cons of both types of jobs.

The Allure of Working Remotely

There are a lot of reasons for some countries to support remote jobs, as one of those traits is being able to work from comfortable places. Being able to work from home or anywhere else allows the employees to take a break from the everyday commute to their workplaces, which allows the workers to focus more on the job. At the same time, remote jobs allow the employee not to be always on the desk like a traditional desk job unnecessarily, which actually improves physical wellbeing and even morale between employees. So for the betterment of the employees and wellbeing and flexibility related to work a remote job is always better.

The Power of the Office

There are some potential reasons too why a desk job is considered superior in some countries. The most crucial reason being not all jobs are compatible for remote jobs. Any job that's not meant to be done remotely is really hard to complete in this dynamic. Another important factor that makes those countries prefer office jobs is connectivity. When people work remotely they feel less connected to their coworkers. This issue completely mitigates when jobs are done in an office.

Final Thoughts

To conclude, in my opinion, there might be some downsides to working a remote job, but the demerits are exacerbated.
