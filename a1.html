The graph illustrates the shifting dynamics in how people accessed music from 2011 to 2018, showing percentages for three main methods: CDs, Downloads, and Streams.

For CDs, usage gradually decreased. In 2011, CDs dominated at around 55% of the market, but by 2018, they held only about 20%.

Downloads started at 35% in 2011, peaked at 40% around 2014, leading the market briefly, but were surpassed by streaming in 2017, dropping to 30% by 2018.

Streaming began as a niche with just 5% in 2011, but grew rapidly, becoming the most popular method by 2018 at 45% of the market.

Overall, the music market shifted dramatically, with streaming rising to prominence while CDs lost ground, and downloads remained somewhat stable after an initial rise.
