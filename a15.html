When Half Failed but Hope Passed - The HSC Story of Sylhet

Last week, the HSC examination results were published, and the news spread across Sylhet faster than a CNG during rush hour. Many students gathered in front of computer shops, nervously refreshing the education board website. When the results appeared, silence filled the streets. Nearly 50% of Sylhet's students had failed.

For a moment, the city felt heartbroken. Parents sighed, teachers frowned, and some Students quietly deleted their Facebook accounts. But among the disappointment, stories of hope began to rise just like the Surma River after heavy rain.

At AIMS English, Najmul Amin Sir, known for his motivational reading sessions, decided to hold a "Special Hope Class." He told his students, "Failure is not the end; it's the best feedback life gives you for free.

." He reminded them that many successful people once failed too-Albert Einstein, J.K. Rowling, even Sylhet's own entrepreneurs who started with nothing but a dream and a tea stall.

One student, Rafi, had failed in two subjects. He confessed that he felt ashamed to face his family. But during the session, he realized that failure wasn't a certificate of defeat— it was an invitation to grow. He started planning to retake the exam and join the AIMS Reading Club to strengthen his English. His friends joined him, turning their disappointment into determination.

Over the next week, the same Sylhet that once looked gloomy started to buzz with new energy. Banners appeared that read, "Try Again, Sylhet!" Students began studying at cafes, on rooftops, and even under umbrellas during power cuts. The city's rhythm changed-from regret to resilience.

In the end, the HSC result might have broken some hearts, but it also built stronger minds. Because sometimes, when half the students fail, the other half learns how to rise.
